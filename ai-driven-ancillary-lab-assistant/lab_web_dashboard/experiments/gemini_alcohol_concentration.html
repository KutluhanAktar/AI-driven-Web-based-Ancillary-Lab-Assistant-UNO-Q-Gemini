Alcohol Usage in Laboratories

Alcohols (Ethanol, Isopropanol, and Methanol) are the "Swiss Army Knives" of the lab. Their primary uses include:

  • Sterilization: 70% Ethanol is the gold standard for disinfecting surfaces and equipment.
  • Solvents: Used to dissolve organic compounds that are not water-soluble.
  • Fixatives: Essential in histology for preserving tissue samples.
  • Fuel: Used in spirit lamps for localized heating.

Dangers & Safety

While useful, alcohol presents significant risks if unmonitored:

  • Flammability: Most alcohols have low flash points. Vapors can ignite even at a distance from an open flame.
  • Toxicity: Methanol, in particular, is toxic via skin absorption or inhalation.
  • Vapor Accumulation: In poorly ventilated areas, alcohol vapors can reach the Lower Explosive Limit (LEL), turning a small spark into a major explosion.

Why Use a Sensor?

Incorporating a sensor like the Gravity Electrochemical module into a lab environment provides an automated safety layer. It can trigger:

  1. Automated Ventilation: Activating exhaust fans when vapor levels cross a threshold.
  2. Early Leak Detection: Identifying a spilled container before the smell becomes noticeable.
  3. Data Logging: Monitoring air quality trends during sensitive experiments.
